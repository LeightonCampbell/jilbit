The phrase "the children are our future" is whispered in every corner of the world. In Jamaica, for too many, it has become a cruel irony.

## The Reality of Survival

In the shadows of the paradise tourists see, a different cycle exists. It is a cycle fueled by hunger. We see children - sons and daughters - forced into the unthinkable. They are selling the only thing they own, their own bodies, just to put a single meal on a family table.

For these children, the "safety net" is a myth.

- **The Homes:** For many girls, being sent to a state-run children's home is not a rescue - it is a relocation of the abuse.
- **The Streets:** For the boys, there is no roof at all. They fend for themselves in an environment that rewards crime and punishes innocence.

## A Society Looking Away

There is a profound silence where there should be an outcry. When the systems designed to protect the vulnerable wash their hands of the problem, they are not just failing individuals; they are abandoning the future of a nation. To the world, they are statistics. To the state, they are a burden.

To us, they are the reason JILBIT exists.

## The JILBIT Commitment

We refuse to accept that a child's story must end where it began. We believe that the trajectory of a life can be rewritten with three simple, radical acts:

- **A Meal:** Because no child should have to trade their dignity for bread.
- **A Bed:** Because peace begins with a safe place to sleep.
- **A Presence:** A supportive, Christ-centered environment that says, "You are seen, you are loved, and you are not for sale."

## Be A Part of This Mission

Our mission team is heading to Jamaica to provide more than just temporary relief. We are building a foundation of hope.

We are making a real, tangible commitment to change the outcome for every child we meet. But we cannot do it alone. Your support provides the bed, the meal, and the safety these children have been denied for too long.

Jesus Is Lord. Believe It. Then, act on it.
